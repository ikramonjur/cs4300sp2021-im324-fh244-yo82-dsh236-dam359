How your results are generated

When you type into the free text search bar and select other options from the slider buttons and drop down menus, you are creating a search query for our used car database. The search engine returns the most similar used cars that match your preferences. The results shown are generated by running a cosine similarity ranking method on all of the reviews for vehicles in our datafiles; results are sorted by cosine similarity and then average user rating.

Our method takes into account both the free text from the search bar as well as the specifics that you mark on the previous page (2 door cars, electric cars, transmission types, etc.). We use tf-idf vectorization, pickle files, and SVD to facilitate data storage and improve computation time. Stemming was implemented to be sure that the cosine similarity can work with words that have the same base such as "sport" and "sporty".
